# Data Profile — Employment Equity Dashboard

## What this project is built on

Two complementary sources from the Treasury Board Secretariat (TBS), covering both *who is represented* and *how those people experience* the federal workplace.

---

### Source 1: Annual Employment Equity Reports (BT1-28 series) — PDFs

**Files:** `BT1-28-2020-eng.pdf` through `BT1-28-2025-eng.pdf`  
**Publisher:** Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat  
**Coverage:** Fiscal years 2019–20 to 2024–25 (six consecutive years)  
**What one record is:** Aggregated representation statistics for the four EE Act designated groups across the core public administration (CPA).

> **Important:** The BT1-28 PDFs have been pre-processed into structured CSVs (see Source 4 below). Use the CSVs for any data logic — scanning the PDFs directly is slow and error-prone. The 2020 and 2021 PDFs are narrative-format only and have no structured department annexes; department-level data starts at FY2021-22.

**Groups covered and their subgroups:**

| Designated Group | Subgroups in BT1-28 reports |
|------------------|-----------------------------|
| Women | Reported as one group; no subgroups |
| Indigenous Peoples | First Nations, Métis, Inuit (Inuk) |
| Persons with Disabilities | Physical, sensory, cognitive, mental health (varies by year) |
| Racialized persons (formerly Visible Minorities) | Black, South Asian, Chinese, Filipino, Arab, Latin American, Southeast Asian, Korean, Japanese, West Asian, Multiple, Other |

**Key metrics per group:** headcount, representation rate (%), workforce availability (WFA) benchmark, gap to WFA, executive-level representation, new hires, promotions, separations.

**Scope:** Core Public Administration only (~60 departments, FAA Schedule I and IV). Excludes Crown corporations, RCMP, Canadian Armed Forces.

**What it can honestly support:** Longitudinal representation trends (2020–2025), benchmarking against WFA, executive pipeline analysis, year-over-year progress on each designated group and their subgroups.

### Source 2: Public Service Employee Survey Microdata (EEINFODV.csv)
**File:** `EEINFODV.csv` (~1.6 million rows, latin-1 encoded)  
**Publisher:** TBS / Statistics Canada  
**Coverage:** 2020, 2022, and 2024 survey cycles (three data points for trend analysis)  
**What one row is:** One survey question's aggregated response distribution for one demographic group at one organization level.

**Groups and subgroups in this file — far broader than the four EE Act groups:**

| Category | Groups Available |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Gender** | Woman, Man, Another gender |
| **Persons with Disabilities** | Combined + 7 disability-type subgroups: seeing, hearing, speech/communication, sensory/environmental, cognitive, learning, n.i.e. |
| **Indigenous Peoples** | Combined Indigenous; Inuk (Inuit) subgroup; First Nations/Métis suppressed at most department levels |
| **Racialized Persons** | Combined + subgroups: Black, East Asian, Southeast Asian, Arab, South Asian, West Asian, White (comparator), Another racial group |
| **Ethnocultural / Geographic Origin** | 25 regions (Africa sub-regions, Asia sub-regions, Americas, Europe, Oceania) + multi-origin |
| **Religion / Belief System** | Buddhism, Christianity, Hinduism, Indigenous spiritual tradition, Islam, Judaism, Sikhism, Secular, Other |
| **Sexual Orientation** | 2SLGBTQIA+ combined; Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Asexual, Pansexual, Heterosexual, Other |
| **Gender Identity** | Two-Spirit, Transgender, Queer, Questioning, Intersex, Another identity |
| **Organizational Level** | Executives vs. Non-Executives; Anglophone vs. Francophone Executives |

**Key columns used for analysis:** `BYCOND` (group filter), `DESCRIP_E` (group name), `QUESTION` + `TITLE_E` (what was asked), `POSITIVE`/`SCORE100` (response outcomes), `ANSCOUNT` (cell size), `DEPT_E` (department), `INDICATORENG` / `SUBINDICATORENG` (thematic area).

**What it can honestly support:** Satisfaction and experience gaps by demographic group, subgroup, department, and theme — with year-over-year trends across 2020, 2022, and 2024 for questions that appear in all three cycles. Identifying which groups report the widest gaps on harassment, career development, psychological safety, and inclusion — and whether those gaps are narrowing or widening over time.

**Critical note on WFA census year:** `wfa_census` column in `bt1_28_representation.csv` is labelled per row — `"2016 Census"` for FY2021-22, `"2021 Census"` for FY2022-23 onward. Always surface this label alongside any WFA gap comparison.

**Critical note on suppression:** `suppressed_flag = "yes"` rows have `*` in the source PDF for one or more group cells — those cells are NaN in the CSV and should be displayed as "suppressed (*)", not zero.
**File:** `2024 PSES Supporting Documentation-Documents de référence du SAFF 2024.xlsx`  
**Role:** Codebook — question-level metadata, scale anchors (which direction is "favourable"), indicator groupings, and survey methodology for the 2024 cycle.  
**Read this before labelling any POSITIVE score as good or bad.**

## How the sources connect

| Question | Source to use |
|----------|--------------|
| Who is represented, and by how much vs. WFA? — department level | `knowledge/bt1_28_representation.csv` |
| Who is represented, and by how much vs. WFA? — CPA-wide headline | `knowledge/tables/01_cpa_summary.csv` |
| Who is represented, and by how much vs. WFA? — subgroups (Black, Inuit, disability type) | `knowledge/tables/05–09_*.csv` |
| Are those gaps closing year over year (FY2015-16 to FY2024-25)? | `knowledge/tables/02_historical_trend.csv` + subgroup trend tables |
| Is the executive pipeline representative? | `knowledge/tables/03_executive_pipeline.csv` |
| What are workforce flows (hires, promotions, separations) by group? | `knowledge/tables/04_workforce_flows.csv` |
| How do designated group employees experience the workplace? | EEINFODV.csv (suppression-guard applied) |
| Which disability type or racial subgroup has the worst experience score? | EEINFODV.csv (EEDV codes) |
| Which departments have the widest experience gaps for a given group or subgroup? | EEINFODV.csv × `bt1_28_representation.csv` (joined by dept name) |
| How do 2SLGBTQIA+, religion, or ethnocultural origin groups experience the workplace? | EEINFODV.csv (experience only — no WFA comparison exists) |
| Where representation has improved, has experience improved too? | BT1-28 CSVs + EEINFODV combined |
| What WFA benchmark applies in a given fiscal year? | `knowledge/tables/14_wfa_benchmarks.csv` or `wfa_census` column in `bt1_28_representation.csv` |

The dashboard covers **both layers for both levels**:
- **Main groups + subgroups** within the four EE Act designated groups: representation (WFA benchmarking) and experience.
- **Extended PSES groups** (2SLGBTQIA+, religion, ethnocultural origin, gender identity): experience scores only — no WFA benchmark exists for these groups.

The dashboard serves two named users with different zoom levels:
- **TBS Chief Human Resources Officer / EE policy team** — portfolio view across all departments; uses the dashboard to decide which departments need system-wide intervention and which designated group or subgroup to prioritize.
- **Deputy Minister / departmental EE coordinator** — single-department view; uses the dashboard to focus the department's annual EE action plan on the right group.

The dashboard's unique value is the **intersection**: surfacing departments and subgroups where representation looks acceptable on paper but experience scores signal systemic problems — the thing neither user can see today without combining two separate data sources manually.

## What this data cannot support

- Causal claims: the data shows correlations and gaps, not why they exist.
- Federal-government-wide statements: BT1-28 scope is CPA only.
- Multi-year PSES trends for questions that changed wording between cycles — verify question comparability before plotting any trend.
- Precise First Nations / Métis department-level comparisons: ANSCOUNT suppression makes these cells unreliable in EEINFODV.
- WFA benchmarks for 2SLGBTQIA+, religion, or ethnocultural origin groups: no census-based WFA exists for these groups.
